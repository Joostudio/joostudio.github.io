print design

Carefully curated color palettes meet bold typographic play.

Overview

I enjoy creating print designs that explore the power of constraints. Working with limited color palettes pushes me to be more intentional, while playful layouts let the visuals dance. From music festival posters to book covers and brochures, my focus is always on clarity, rhythm, and visual impact.

Posters

Nomination for London International Creative Competition 2018

This is children’s book, “Find Me!” for under age 10. The storyline is that the little kid wanted to play hide and seek with his mom. The boy hided and his mom looked for her son. While she was looking for her son, she find the several “cute crime scenes”. His mom pretended not to find him to surprise her son. Finally she found her son and the boy was very excited. He wanted to play it again.

Book Cover

Art Center Gallery | Spring 2019

Dinner Party : This project is based on one of colorchip names, “Dinner Party.” I designed the book cover based on my imaginary story using only 3 given colors. The food on the plate is not just food. The shape of food is a human figure. One of the people in this dinner party will be murdered by someone. Who will be killed? Who will be the murderer? I made a title sequence based on this storyline.
