iOrderfoods

Improving a live ordering platform through practical UX thinking and cross-platform insights.

Overview

iOrderFoods is a live online ordering platform that helps restaurants efficiently manage pickup orders. It replaces phone-based ordering, reducing wait times, minimizing errors, and lowering labor costs. As a product-focused UI/UX designer, I led enhancements across the customer-facing experience and the internal management interface. My goal was to reduce friction in key flows, improve clarity, and support operational efficiency—without interrupting active users.

My Role

User Research / Visual Design / Prototyping & Testing
I conducted competitor research not to mimic others, but to clarify how our product could stand out. I found that many food-ordering apps shared the same UX pitfalls. Instead of following those patterns, I pulled proven solutions from more mature digital products and adapted them for our users' real needs. Since the product was already live with active users, I prioritized speed and practicality—skipping wireframes and rapidly prototyping solutions based on familiar interaction models.

What I Worked On

• Interviewed users and reviewed customer support cases to uncover usability gaps
• Conducted competitor analysis to define opportunities for differentiation
• Applied cross-platform UX patterns for fast, intuitive improvements:
      iMessage → for improving order notification visibility
      Nordstrom app → for embedding help documentation into the admin interface
      Amazon, GAP, Rag & Bone → for persistent and attention-grabbing checkout CTAs
• Designed and tested high-fidelity prototypes in Figma
• Improved platform usability across mobile, tablet, and desktop environments

Key UX Enhancements

1. E-commerce & Online Ordering System

1.1. Accessible Login



Problem: New users were confused by the login flow
Solution:Made the login screen appear immediately after clicking “Order,” reducing hesitation and entry friction



1.2. Persistent Checkout CTA



Problem: Customers often thought they had completed an order without finalizing payment
Insight: Online food ordering is a relatively new UX pattern, so I analyzed e-commerce leaders like Amazon and GAP.
I noticed that Amazon's CTA button is deliberately disruptive (yellow on gray) to prevent missed action. GAP and Rag & Bone also keep checkout buttons persistently visible.
Solution:I applied this strategy by keeping the “Pay” button clearly visible until payment was confirmed



1.3. Visual Menu Tags



Problem: Dietary info (e.g., spicy, veggie) and featured items weren’t distinguishable
Design: Added visual icons and color tags to improve scannability and item clarity



1.4. Support Portal


Problem: Basic usage questions were generating frequent support calls
Design: Created a help center with documentation, embedded in the admin interface → Support calls dropped by ~90%



1.5. Admin Guide Access



Problem: Users couldn’t easily find onboarding materials or remember the help URL
Insight: I originally proposed linking the guide based on my past website management experience. When my manager asked for proof of similar industry use, I researched and found Nordstrom’s contextual help integration. I shared the example with the developer, and it led directly to implementation.
Solution:Embedded a permanent help link inside the admin menu



2. Order Management Improvements

2.1. Improved Notification Visibility



Problem: New or unread orders weren’t visually clear, leading to missed tickets
Insight: Inspired by iMessage, I used familiar visual cues (bold text, red dots, persistent alerts) to improve urgency and visibility
Design Solution:Highlighted unread orders using attention-grabbing UI patterns



2.2. Real-Time Order Controls


Problem: Staff couldn’t easily modify delivery times or toggle online ordering
Design Solution:Added toggle switches and sliders for in-app control without needing admin panel access


2.3. Streamlined Admin Access


Problem: Users often confused the order app with the main admin system
Design Solution:Added a direct shortcut to the full admin dashboard via the hamburger menu


Results

• Support call volume reduced by ~90%
• Checkout abandonment significantly decreased
• Increased clarity and confidence among restaurant owners and staff
• Reduced onboarding friction and training time
